---

Federation in a GCP context refers to a trust relationship established between two entities to enable them to share resources securely. However, this trust relationship can be abused. For instance, an attacker can manipulate tokens in the federation process, impersonating a trusted entity to gain unauthorized access to resources. This is often achieved by exploiting vulnerabilities in the federation process or through social engineering tactics. Therefore, it's crucial for organizations to have strong security measures in place, including regular audits of federated identities, adherence to the principle of least privilege, and robust monitoring and logging practices.
